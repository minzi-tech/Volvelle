The board is veiled. Work by touch — the gates lie at the cardinal points, the ring between hub and rim. Each sounding is a chime; the record listens, and will be shown when the sitting closes.

The record is empty. Rest a finger on the board; hold upon a letter to sound it.

The grammar

Planchette — rest one finger, or several; the planchette rides their midpoint. Two sitters, one board.

Sounding — hold upon a character until the ring fills. For a doubled letter, circle off and return. Linger well beyond the sounding and the letter kindles — emphasis, marked (!) in the record.

Declarative — sweep along the ring: plain speech. It takes no mark.

Vocative — move inward toward Ain to address the essential (˄); outward toward the rim, the manifest (˅).

Interrogative — a spiral, changing angle and depth at once, marks the question (?).

Seal — close a loop to end the utterance (⁘).

Negation — retrace your path back upon itself to unsay the last character.

Breath — lift all hands for the space between words.

Key — turn the ring between utterances; every turning is recorded as a modulation.

Gates — Open begins the record; Close seals the sitting; Name gilds the word that follows; a dwell upon Ain is deliberate silence (…).

Eyes closed — the board is veiled and the record hidden; only the chime confirms each sounding. Work by touch, and let the sitting reveal itself when it closes.

Stream — the dwell is lifted; every character the planchette crosses is sounded. Raw speech, to be parsed after, as the old circles did.

Journal — write your question above the record before you begin. When the sitting closes, question, key, mode, and record are kept together; add your notes upon them after.

The attendant — Listen takes your question by voice; Attend summons a fixed interrogation (attendant v1) that refines it aloud: the form of answer, its season, its watchtower. Voice hearing requires the page be served over https; the attendant's questions may always be answered by touch.
